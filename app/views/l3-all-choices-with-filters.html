  • Apprenticeships

    Apprenticeships are real jobs that allow you to earn a wage while you learn. They can take between 1 and 5 years to complete, depending on the level.

  • Skills Bootcamps

    Free flexible training courses for adults to develop specific skills in digital, technical, construction and green sectors, with a guaranteed job interview at the end.

  • T levels

    T Levels are nationally-recognised qualifications for 16 to 19-year-olds that takes 2 years to complete.

  • Vocational Technical Qualifications (VTQs)

    Vocational Technical Qualifications (VTQs) are practical qualifications for over 16s. They're designed to help you get the skills you need to start your career or go on to higher levels of education.

  • Find a job

    Find full or part-time work using the Find A Job service to search and apply for jobs in England, Scotland and Wales.

  • Supported internships if you have an education, health and care plan

    Supported internships are an unpaid work-based study programme that usually lasts for one year. It includes an extended work placement that lasts for at least 6 months.

  • School leaver schemes

    School leavers scheme is offered by some companies to young people who have completed A levels. The schemes allow you to learn and train with a large company while earning a wage.

  • Higher Technical Qualifications (HTQs)

    Higher Technical Qualifications (HTQs) are technical qualifications that employers have helped develop so you can get the right training and skills you need to succeed at work.

  • Multiply

    Improving your numeracy could help you in your everyday life, from budgeting money to finding a new job.

  • Free courses for jobs

    Hundreds of free courses available for those aged 19 and over, across different sectors, from accounting or construction to courses in early years, digital, catering, adult care and more.

  • Digital essential skills

    Free Digital Essential skills qualifications to develop the essential digital skills needed for work and everyday life.

  • English essential skills

    Taking a free English essential skills course can improve your communication skills, helping you progress in work and everyday life.

  • Numeracy essential skills

    These courses will help you gain the numeracy skills you need for work and everyday life and are recognised by employers.

  • The Skills Toolkit

    Flexible courses open to everyone to boost your skills at your own pace in digital, numeracy and transferable skills.

  • A levels

    A levels are subject-based qualifications usually assessed by exams. They can lead to further study, training or work.

  • Higher education

    Higher eduction is the name for qualifications and courses you can take after 18. It includes diplomas, foundation years, bachelor degrees and more.
