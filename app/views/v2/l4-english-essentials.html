English essential skills

Taking a free English essential skills course can improve your communication skills, helping you progress in work and everyday life. 

What are English essential skills courses?

If you are looking to improve your English, there are two qualifications you can take: 

English Essential Skills courses can improve your communication skills, helping you progress in work and everyday life.  

English courses for speakers of other languages (ESOL) are also available to help you get the essential skills needed for life and work in the UK. 

 In these qualifications you will learn how to use English for: 

  • reading
  • writing
  • speaking
  • listening 

All these skills are needed by employers, so boosting yours could help you take the next step to a better job.  

They can also help you with everyday life in the UK such as shopping, finding work or registering with a doctor.  

All courses lead to a recognised certificate, whether it is an entry-level course, or a level 1 or 2 course (equivalent to GCSE). 

Who can take an English essential skills course?

The courses are available to adults aged 19 and over, living in England who do not have a GCSE in English at grade C (equivalent to grade 4 or CSE grade 1).  

English Essential Skills courses are free.   

ESOL courses may be free or part-funded depending on your level of income or employment status. 

Where can I do an English essential skills course?

Courses take 3 to 6 months to complete and can be classroom-based or online.   

Full-time, part-time and evening class options may also be available, depending on your local provider.
