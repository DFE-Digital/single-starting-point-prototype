Supported internships with an education, health and care plan

Supported internships are an unpaid work-based study programme for those aged 16 to 24 that usually lasts for one year. It includes an extended work placement that lasts for at least 6 months.

Qualification Level:

Informal with no qualification

Cost:

Free

How long they take:

6 to 12 months

Benefits:

They help you to secure and sustain employment through learning in the workplace, with support from a qualified job coach.

Apprenticeships

Apprenticeships are real jobs that allow you to earn a wage while you learn. They can take between 1 and 5 years to complete, depending on the level.

Qualification Level:

Level 2 to Level 7 (degree apprenticeship)

Cost:

You are paid the apprentice or national minimum wage depending on age

How long they take:

1 to 5 years depending on the level

Benefits:

You work with experienced staff and learn skills relevant to your chosen profession, and get time for training or study during the working week (at least 20% of your normal working hours)

Skills Bootcamps

Free flexible training courses for adults aged 19 and over, to develop specific skills in digital, technical, construction and green sectors, with a guaranteed job interview at the end.

Qualification Level:

Level 2 to level 5

Cost:

Free government funded scheme

How long they take:

16 weeks

Benefits:

Gives you opportunity to build up valuable sector-specific skills, based on local employer demand and provides a direct path to a job on completion.

Higher Technical Qualifications (HTQs)

Qualification Level:

Cost:

Cost can vary depending on the course studied

How long they take:

1 to 2 years

Benefits:

They help you develop the right knowledge, skills and behaviors employers need.

A levels

For those aged 16 and over, A levels are subject-based qualifications usually assessed by exams. They can lead to further study, training or work.

Qualification Level:

Level 3

Cost:

Free

How long they take:

2 years

Benefits:

They help you develop skills needed in particular subject areas and allow you to move on to an apprenticeship, join a school leavers scheme, find another type of higher education or go straight into employment.
