School leaver schemes

Learn and train with a large company while earning a wage. 

What is a school leavers scheme?

Some companies offer school leaver schemes to young people who have completed A levels. The schemes allow you to learn and train with a large company while earning a wage. 

Schemes are offered by employers in sectors such as accounting and finance, engineering, law, leisure and retail.

Employers create their own schemes, so each one will be slightly different.

Some of things your scheme might include:

  • following a training programme 
  • earn a qualification, but not always
  • rotate between different locations with the same company
  • complete training online, in college or at a university

Once you’ve finished a school leaver scheme, you could continue your training with an apprenticeship or higher education qualification.   

You could also get a paid job at the same place you trained with or you could look for a job with a different employer.  

Who can do a school leavers scheme?

To start a school leaver scheme, you’ll need to: 

  • be 18 or over
  • not be in full time education

Usually, the schemes require high A level grades or equivalent qualifications. 

You can apply for a school leavers scheme while you’re still at school. 

Where can I do a school leavers scheme?

Schemes usually take place wherever the company is based. This could be an office, a lab 

Find a school leavers scheme

You’ll need to check each company’s website to see if they offer a school leaver scheme and how to apply.
