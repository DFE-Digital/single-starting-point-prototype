Vocational technical qualifications (VTQs)

VTQs are practical qualifications for over 16s. They're designed to help you get the skills you need to start your career or go on to higher levels of education. 

What is a VTQ?

VTQs focus on a specific job or a broad employment area, such as childcare, engineering or IT. There are over 2,000 different subjects available across the different VTQs. 

They might be for you if you have a strong interest in working in a particular industry.

There are a few different types and levels of VTQs, including:

  • BTECs: level 1 to 7 qualifications
  • Cambridge Nationals: level 1 and 2 qualifications
  • Cambridge Technicals: level 2 and 3 qualifications
  • T Levels: level 3 qualifications

The length of the VTQ will be different for each one, but the minimum length is one year. 

After completing a VTQ, you could go straight into employment or start another type of higher education. 

Who can take a VTQ?

To start a VTQ, you’ll need to: 

  • be 16 or over
  • live in England
  • not be in full time education

Where can I do a VTQ?

VTQs usually take place at a school or college. Each educational provider sets their own entry requirements. The grades you’ll need to have will depend on the level and type of course you’re applying for. 

Find a VTQ

You can search for colleges and schools currently offering VTQ courses to find out where your nearest one is.
